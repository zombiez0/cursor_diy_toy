üî¨ Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Prepare Your Workspace

Cover your work surface with newspaper or plastic. Have all materials within reach and wear old clothes - slime can be messy! Wash your hands thoroughly before starting.

Pro Tip: Work on a surface that's easy to clean, like a kitchen counter or table with a tablecloth.
2

Pour the Base Glue

Empty the entire 4 oz bottle of white school glue into your large mixing bowl. Make sure to get every drop out by squeezing the bottle and tapping it upside down.

Science Fact: The glue contains polymers - long chains of molecules that will give our slime its stretchy properties!
3

Add Fluffy Shaving Cream

Add 2-3 pumps of white shaving cream to the glue. This will make your slime extra fluffy and cloud-like. Gently stir with a spoon to mix it in.

Fun Fact: Shaving cream contains tiny air bubbles that make the slime lighter and more fun to squish!
4

Create the Activator Solution

In a small cup, mix 1 tablespoon of contact lens solution with 1/2 teaspoon of baking soda. Stir well until the baking soda dissolves completely. This is your "activator"!

Important: The contact lens solution MUST contain boric acid for this to work. Check the ingredients list!
5

Divide Into Rainbow Sections

Divide your white glue mixture into 5-6 small bowls (one for each rainbow color). You should have roughly equal amounts in each bowl.

Rainbow Order: Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, Purple - just like a real rainbow!
6

Add Beautiful Colors

Add 3-4 drops of food coloring to each bowl - red in one, yellow in another, blue in the next, etc. Stir each bowl until the color is evenly mixed throughout.

Color Mixing: Don't have orange? Mix red and yellow! No purple? Mix red and blue!
7

Activate Each Color

Add about 1/2 teaspoon of your activator solution to the first colored bowl. Stir quickly - you'll see the slime start to form immediately! The mixture will pull away from the sides of the bowl.

Chemistry Magic: The boric acid in the contact solution cross-links the polymer chains, creating slime!
8

Knead Each Color Section

Take the slime out of the bowl and knead it with your hands for 2-3 minutes. It might be sticky at first, but keep kneading! Add tiny amounts of activator if it's too sticky.

Texture Check: Perfect slime should be stretchy, not sticky, and shouldn't leave residue on your hands.
9

Repeat for All Colors

Repeat steps 7 and 8 for each of your colored bowls. You'll end up with 5-6 separate colored slimes. Take your time - each color needs individual attention!

Patience Pays: Don't rush this step. Well-kneaded slime has the best texture and stretch!
10

Create the Rainbow Effect

Gently stretch each colored slime into a long rope. Place them side by side in rainbow order, then carefully twist or braid them together. Don't overmix - you want to keep the rainbow pattern!

Art Technique: Try making spirals, waves, or even a rainbow heart shape with your colored slimes!
11

Add Optional Glitter

If using glitter, sprinkle it lightly over your rainbow slime and gently fold it in. A little goes a long way - you want sparkle, not a glitter explosion!

Glitter Tip: Fine glitter works better than chunky glitter and feels nicer to touch.
12

Play and Store

Your rainbow slime is ready! Stretch it, poke it, watch the colors swirl together. When done playing, store in an airtight container to keep it fresh for weeks!

Storage Secret: Add a tiny bit of lotion if your slime gets stiff after storage. It'll become stretchy again!
