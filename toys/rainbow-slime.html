üåà Rainbow Slime Recipe

Create amazing rainbow slime that stretches, bounces, and sparkles! Learn about polymers while making this colorful, safe slime perfect for sensory play.

üë∂ Age: 4+ years
‚è±Ô∏è Time: 15 minutes
üìä Difficulty: Beginner
üß™ Category: Science

üõçÔ∏è Materials Needed

White School Glue

4 oz bottle (Elmer's recommended)

Shaving Cream

2-3 pumps (foam type)

Contact Lens Solution

Must contain boric acid

Food Coloring

Various colors (gel or liquid)

Mixing Bowls

5-6 small bowls for colors

Wooden Spoons

For mixing each color

üìã Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Prepare Your Workspace

Cover your work surface with newspaper or plastic. Gather all materials and wash your hands thoroughly. Set up 5-6 small bowls for different colors.

üí° Pro Tip: Use disposable bowls to make cleanup easier!
2

Divide the Glue

Pour about 2/3 of the white glue into each bowl. You'll need roughly equal amounts for each color you want to make.

‚ö†Ô∏è Safety: Adult supervision recommended for younger children
3

Add Food Coloring

Add 2-3 drops of different food coloring to each bowl. Use red, orange, yellow, green, blue, and purple to make a rainbow!

üß™ Science Fact: Food coloring molecules spread through the glue by diffusion!
4

Mix Colors Thoroughly

Stir each bowl with a separate spoon until the color is evenly distributed. Make sure there are no streaks of white glue remaining.

5

Add Shaving Cream

Add 2-3 pumps of shaving cream to each bowl. This makes the slime fluffy and stretchy!

üß™ Science Fact: Shaving cream contains surfactants that create tiny air bubbles!
6

Mix Shaving Cream

Gently fold the shaving cream into each colored glue mixture. Don't overmix - you want to keep the air bubbles!

7

Add Contact Solution

Add contact lens solution one tablespoon at a time to each bowl. The mixture will start to pull away from the sides.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important: Contact solution must contain boric acid to work as an activator
8

Mix Until Slime Forms

Keep stirring until the mixture becomes stretchy and starts pulling away from the bowl. Add more contact solution if needed.

üß™ Science Fact: Boric acid creates cross-links between polymer chains, making slime!
9

Knead Each Color

Take each colored slime out of its bowl and knead it with your hands for 1-2 minutes until it's smooth and stretchy.

10

Create Rainbow Layers

Flatten each colored slime and layer them in rainbow order: red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple.

11

Fold and Twist

Gently fold the layered slime in half and twist it to create beautiful rainbow swirls. Don't overmix or colors will blend!

12

Play and Store

Your rainbow slime is ready! Stretch it, poke it, and watch the colors swirl. Store in an airtight container when not playing.

üí° Storage Tip: Keep slime in the refrigerator to make it last longer!

üîß Troubleshooting Guide

Problem: Slime is too sticky

Solution: Add more contact lens solution, one tablespoon at a time, and knead well.

Problem: Slime is too stiff

Solution: Add a small amount of warm water or lotion and knead gently.

Problem: Colors are muddy

Solution: Use less food coloring next time, or separate colors better during mixing.

Problem: Slime won't form

Solution: Check that your contact solution contains boric acid - this is essential for activation.
