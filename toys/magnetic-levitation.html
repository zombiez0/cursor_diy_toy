üîß Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Understand Magnetic Poles

Test how magnets attract and repel each other

Before building, experiment with your magnets. Hold two magnets close together and feel how they either attract (pull together) or repel (push apart). Mark the poles with tape so you know which sides repel.

üí° Physics Lesson:

  • Like poles repel (North-North or South-South)
  • Opposite poles attract (North-South)
  • We'll use repulsion to create levitation!
2

Create the Base Structure

Build a stable frame to hold your levitation system

Cut your cardboard into a 12" x 12" square. Mark four points in a square pattern, each 3" from the center. Carefully poke holes and insert wooden dowels to create a frame about 6" tall.

üí° Pro Tips:

  • Make sure dowels are perfectly vertical
  • Test stability by gently shaking the frame
  • Add tape at the base for extra security
3

Position Base Magnets

Arrange base magnets with same poles facing up

Tape four magnets to the cardboard base, positioning them in a square pattern. Make sure all magnets have the SAME pole facing upward (all North or all South). This creates a repulsive field above the base.

üí° Critical Step:

  • Double-check all poles are the same direction
  • Use strong tape to secure magnets firmly
  • Space magnets evenly for balanced field
4

Create the Floating Platform

Build a lightweight platform with repelling magnets

Cut a small cardboard square (3" x 3") for your floating platform. Attach 2-3 magnets underneath with the SAME poles facing DOWN as your base magnets face UP. This creates repulsion between platform and base.

üí° Balance Tips:

  • Keep platform as light as possible
  • Center the magnets for stability
  • Test with different magnet arrangements
5

Add Guide System

Create guides to prevent the platform from drifting sideways

Carefully cut small holes in your floating platform that allow the dowels to pass through loosely. The dowels act as guides, preventing the platform from drifting sideways while allowing it to float up and down.

üí° Precision Tips:

  • Holes should be slightly larger than dowels
  • Sand holes smooth to reduce friction
  • Test fit before final assembly
6

Test Your Levitation!

Watch in amazement as your platform floats in mid-air!

Carefully place your floating platform on the dowel guides above the base magnets. If positioned correctly, the platform should float in mid-air, held up by magnetic repulsion!

üí° Troubleshooting:

  • If platform falls, check magnet pole alignment
  • Adjust height by sliding platform on dowels
  • Add or remove magnets to change floating height
7

Add Floating Objects

Place lightweight objects on your floating platform

Once your platform is stable, try placing small, lightweight objects on it. Paper clips, coins, small toys, or even a piece of paper can appear to float in mid-air!

üí° Experiment Ideas:

  • Test different object weights
  • Try magnetic vs non-magnetic objects
  • See how many objects you can float at once

üß™ The Physics of Magnetic Levitation

üî¨ How Does It Work?

Your levitation device demonstrates several important physics principles:

üß≤ Magnetic Fields

Every magnet creates an invisible magnetic field around it. When two magnets with like poles face each other, their fields push against each other, creating a repulsive force.

‚öñÔ∏è Force Balance

Your platform floats because the upward magnetic force exactly balances the downward gravitational force. It's like an invisible hand holding the platform up!

üöÑ Real-World Applications

Magnetic levitation is used in:

  • Maglev Trains: Float above tracks for smooth, fast travel
  • Magnetic Bearings: Reduce friction in machines
  • Levitating Displays: Make objects appear to float
