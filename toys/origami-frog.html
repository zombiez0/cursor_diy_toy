üê∏ Jumping Origami Frog

Create an amazing paper frog that actually jumps when you press its back! This classic origami project combines fun with learning as kids discover geometry, following directions, and cause-and-effect.

üìã Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Prepare Your Paper

Start with a square piece of paper, colored side down on your work surface. Make sure your hands are clean and the surface is flat.

üí° Pro Tip: If you don't have square paper, you can make one by folding a rectangular piece diagonally and cutting off the excess.
2

Make Diagonal Folds

Fold the paper in half diagonally both ways to create an X pattern, then unfold completely. These creases will guide your next folds.

üí° Pro Tip: Press firmly on the creases to make them sharp and clear.
3

Create the Base

Fold the paper in half horizontally, then push the sides in to form a triangle base. This is called a "waterbomb base" in origami.

üí° Pro Tip: The colored side should now be on the outside of your triangle.
4

Form the Front Legs

Take the front flaps and fold them up to the top point of the triangle. These will become your frog's front legs.

üí° Pro Tip: Make sure both sides are even and symmetrical.
5

Create the Frog's Arms

Fold the outer corners of the front legs outward to create the frog's arms. Adjust the angle to make them look natural.

üí° Pro Tip: The arms should stick out at about a 45-degree angle.
6

Flip and Prepare Back Legs

Flip the frog over. You'll see a diamond shape. Fold the bottom corners up to the center line to start forming the back legs.

üí° Pro Tip: Keep the folds neat and aligned with the center line.
7

Shape the Back Legs

Fold the angled edges outward to create the frog's powerful back legs. These legs will give your frog its jumping ability!

üí° Pro Tip: The wider you make the back legs, the better your frog will jump.
8

Create the Jumping Mechanism

Fold the bottom edge up about 1/3 of the way, then fold it back down to create a small pleat. This creates the spring action!

üí° Pro Tip: The pleat should be about 1/4 inch wide for the best jumping action.
9

Flip and Adjust

Flip your frog over so it's sitting right-side up. Gently press down on the frog's back to test the jumping mechanism.

üí° Pro Tip: If it doesn't jump well, adjust the pleat to make it tighter or looser.
10

Decorate Your Frog

Use markers to draw eyes, spots, or patterns on your frog. Make it unique and give it personality!

üí° Pro Tip: Draw the eyes on the front point of the frog for the most realistic look.
11

Test the Jump

Place your frog on a flat surface. Press down gently on the back pleat and quickly release. Watch your frog jump!

üí° Pro Tip: Try different surfaces - smooth tables work better than carpet for jumping.
12

Create a Frog Family

Make multiple frogs in different sizes and colors. Have jumping competitions to see which frog can jump the farthest!

üí° Pro Tip: Smaller frogs often jump farther than larger ones due to their lighter weight.

üîß Troubleshooting

‚ùì Frog Won't Jump

Solution: Check the pleat at the back - it should be tight and springy. Also ensure the back legs are spread wide enough.

‚ùì Folds Won't Stay

Solution: Press creases firmly with your fingernail. If paper is too thick, try thinner origami paper.

‚ùì Frog Looks Lopsided

Solution: Make sure all folds are symmetrical. Unfold and re-fold any uneven sections.
