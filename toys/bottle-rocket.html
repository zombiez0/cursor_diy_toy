üîß Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Prepare Your Workspace

Find a clean, well-lit workspace with room to spread out materials. Cover the surface with newspaper and have adult supervision ready for cutting steps. Wash hands before starting!

Safety First: Adult supervision required for all cutting tasks and rocket launching!
2

Clean and Prepare the Bottle

Thoroughly clean your 2-liter plastic bottle with warm soapy water. Remove all labels and adhesive residue. Dry completely inside and out. Check for any cracks or damage.

Eco Tip: You're giving this bottle a second life instead of throwing it away! üåç
3

Design and Cut the Fins

Draw 3-4 triangular fins on cardboard, each about 4 inches tall and 3 inches wide at the base. Make them identical for balanced flight. Cut carefully with scissors.

Physics Fact: Fins stabilize the rocket's flight by creating drag at the back, keeping the nose pointed forward!
4

Attach the Fins

Space the fins evenly around the bottom section of the bottle (the end opposite the cap). Use duct tape to secure each fin firmly. Make sure they're straight and aligned.

Pro Tip: Use a marker to mark equal spacing around the bottle before attaching fins for perfect symmetry.
5

Create the Nose Cone

Cut a half-circle from cardboard (about 6 inches diameter) and roll it into a cone shape. The cone should fit over the neck of your bottle. Tape the seam securely.

Aerodynamics: A pointed nose cone reduces air resistance, helping your rocket fly higher and farther!
6

Add Soft Nose Padding

Stuff soft foam or bubble wrap into the nose cone for safety. This cushioning protects people and property when the rocket lands. Secure with tape.

Safety Essential: Never skip the padding! This makes your rocket safe for outdoor fun.
7

Attach the Nose Cone

Carefully slide the padded nose cone over the bottle's neck area. Secure with several wraps of duct tape, making sure it's firmly attached but the bottle opening remains accessible.

Test Check: Gently shake the rocket - nothing should rattle or feel loose!
8

Prepare the Cork Launch System

Test fit your cork in the bottle opening - it should go in snugly but not so tight that you can't remove it. If too loose, wrap with tape. If too tight, sand lightly.

Perfect Fit: The cork needs to hold pressure but pop out when pressure builds up enough for launch!
9

Create Launch Tube

Push the pump needle through the center of the cork. The needle should go all the way through but not stick out the other side. This creates your pressure system.

Adult Help: Have an adult help with this step to ensure the needle goes through safely and straight.
10

Set Up Launch Area

Choose a large, open outdoor area away from windows, cars, and people. The rocket can fly 50+ feet! Mark a clear launch zone and set up your rocket pointing away from any obstacles.

Location Tips: Parks, large backyards, or empty fields work best. Avoid windy days for best results!
11

Load and Launch!

Fill bottle 1/3 with water, insert cork firmly, connect pump, and start pumping! Count pumps together (usually 10-15). Step back and watch your rocket soar when pressure pops the cork!

Launch Safety: Everyone stands at least 10 feet away during pumping and launch. Never point at people!
12

Experiment and Improve

Try different amounts of water (1/4, 1/2, 1/3) and different pump pressures. Test how fin shape affects flight. Keep a launch log to track your best flights!

Science Method: Change one variable at a time to see what makes your rocket fly best!

üî¨ The Science Behind Your Rocket

üå™Ô∏è Air Pressure

When you pump air into the bottle, you're increasing air pressure inside. When pressure gets too high, it forces the cork out and pushes water down and out, creating thrust that launches your rocket up!

‚öñÔ∏è Newton's Third Law

"For every action, there's an equal and opposite reaction." Water shoots down, rocket goes up! This is the same principle that powers real rockets into space.

‚úàÔ∏è Aerodynamics

Your nose cone reduces air resistance (drag), and fins provide stability. These design features help your rocket fly straighter and farther, just like real rockets!

‚ôªÔ∏è Recycling Power

By reusing a plastic bottle, you're reducing waste AND creating something amazing! This shows how recycled materials can have exciting second lives.
