üìã Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Start with a Square

Begin with a square piece of colored paper, colored side facing up. Choose bright, beautiful colors that real butterflies might have!

üí° Pro Tip: Patterned origami paper creates stunning butterfly wings with natural-looking designs.
2

Make the First Fold

Fold the paper in half diagonally to create a triangle. Make sure the edges line up perfectly and press the crease firmly.

üí° Pro Tip: Sharp, clean creases are essential for a beautiful butterfly shape.
3

Create the Center Line

Fold the triangle in half again to find the center line, then unfold. This crease will help you align the wings symmetrically.

üí° Pro Tip: The center line is crucial for making both wings identical.
4

Form the Upper Wings

Take the top corners of the triangle and fold them down to meet at the center line. These will become the butterfly's upper wings.

üí° Pro Tip: Make sure both sides are perfectly symmetrical for realistic butterfly wings.
5

Create the Lower Wings

Flip the paper over. Fold the bottom point up to create the lower wings. The tip should extend slightly beyond the upper wings.

üí° Pro Tip: Real butterflies have lower wings that are slightly different from upper wings.
6

Shape the Body

Fold the top point down about 1/3 of the way to create the butterfly's head and body. This gives your butterfly a more realistic shape.

üí° Pro Tip: The body should be proportional to the wings - not too big or too small.
7

Create Wing Definition

Gently fold the wings slightly upward along the center line to give your butterfly a 3D appearance, as if it's ready to fly!

üí° Pro Tip: Don't make the fold too sharp - butterflies have gently curved wings.
8

Add Wing Patterns

Use markers or crayons to add beautiful patterns to the wings. Draw spots, stripes, or create your own unique designs!

üí° Pro Tip: Look at pictures of real butterflies for inspiration, or create fantasy patterns.
9

Draw the Antennae

Add two curved lines from the head to create antennae. These help butterflies smell and navigate in the real world!

üí° Pro Tip: Antennae should be thin and slightly curved, like real butterfly antennae.
10

Add Final Details

Add any final decorative touches - spots on the body, wing borders, or even tiny eyes. Make your butterfly unique!

üí° Pro Tip: Less is often more - don't overcrowd your butterfly with too many details.
